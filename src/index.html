Seattle Housing Prices
An exploration of Seattle's housing prices

Why is an apartment near Union Lake on sale with almost the same price as one big house in Renton? What factors contribute to the vast differences in housing prices?



In this Explorable Explanation, we would like to gain insight into housing in Seattle and other metro cities in the US and analyze the factors of housing prices, such as traffic, crime stats, nearby supermarkets, and GDP per capita.

Let’s first take a look at economic data.

We can see from the following graph that all four metro cities demonstrate a reasonable correlation between GDP per capita and housing prices. For each city, both the GDP and the housing price curves take a downturn between 2008 and 2012. The curves resume the trend of steady increments after 2012 as the economy recovers.

Now let’s bring all the factors together.

From the above exploration, we’ve looked at the correlation of housing prices with respect to the individual factors. Nevertheless, in the real world, these factors tend to combine with each other and have an aggregated effect on people’s purchasing decisions and preferences.
